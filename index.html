What is Naturalization?

    Naturalization is the legal process by which a foreign-born individual becomes a U.S. citizen. To qualify, applicants must meet specific requirements, such as:

    • Having held lawful permanent resident (LPR) status for a minimum of five years (or three years if married to a U.S. citizen)

    • Demonstrating continuous residence and physical presence in the U.S.

    • Showing good moral character

    • Passing the U.S. citizenship test, which includes English language proficiency and knowledge of U.S. history and government

    For many, the naturalization process involves submitting a detailed application, preparing for an interview, and completing the citizenship test all of which can be daunting without the right support.
